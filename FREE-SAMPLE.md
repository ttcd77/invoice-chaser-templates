## Template 1 — Gentle Reminder (Day 1 After Due)

**Best sent:** The day after the due date, or up to 2-3 days after if it's a long-term client.

**Subject:** Quick check on invoice [Invoice Number]

**Body:**

> Hi [Client Name],
>
> Just a quick note — invoice [Invoice Number] for [Amount] was due on [Due Date]. I know how easy it is for these to get buried, so I wanted to flag it in case it slipped your inbox.
>
> If you've already sent payment, ignore this and thanks as always. If not, no rush — just wanted to make sure it landed on your end.
>
> Cheers,
> [Your Name]

**Why this works:** This email assumes good faith. It gives the client an easy out ("maybe it's already paid, maybe the invoice got lost") and doesn't create pressure. The word "quick" in the subject line signals this isn't a big deal — yet. You want the client to think "oh right, let me sort that" rather than "ugh, they're already chasing me."

**The result I've seen:** About 70% of late invoices get paid within 48 hours of sending this. Most people genuinely forgot. You don't need to be aggressive — you just need to be present.

## Why This Approach Works

- **Consistency.** Clients who know they'll get a polite but steady reminder are more likely to pay on time next round.
- **No emotion.** The templates handle the tone so you don't have to think about it when you're already frustrated.
- **Escalation that makes sense.** Each email acknowledges the previous one and moves the conversation forward — it doesn't start from zero every time.
- **Human tone.** These read like a real person wrote them. Not a corporate HR bot, not a collections agency. Just someone who did the work and would like to get paid.

## The 5-Stage System

| Stage | When | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Gentle Reminder | Day 1 after due | Friendly, assumes good faith. "Just checking you got the invoice." |
| 2 — Follow-Up | Day 3-5 | Slightly more direct. Asks for a timeline. |
| 3 — Overdue Notice | Day 7-10 | Tone shifts. "This needs attention." Opens the door for issues. |
| 4 — Escalation | Day 14-21 | Firm but professional. CC their finance department. Sets a deadline. |
| 5 — Final Notice | Day 30+ | Last email before formal action. Unambiguous without being hostile. |

# Revision Policy — One-Page Client Agreement

> **Purpose:** This one-pager sets clear boundaries on revisions. It's designed to be attached to every proposal and SOW. Customize the numbers, keep the principles.

---

## Our Revision Policy

We want you to love the work we deliver. Revisions are how we get there. But unlimited revisions create unlimited delays — and nobody wins when a project drags on for months.

Here's exactly how revisions work on every project:

### What's Included: [2] Revision Rounds

Every project includes **[2] rounds of revisions** on each deliverable at no additional charge.

**A revision round works like this:**

1. We deliver the first version of your [website / design / copy / etc.]
2. You review and send us all your feedback in one batch
3. We make the changes and deliver the updated version
4. You review again and send your final batch of feedback
5. We make those final changes and deliver the finished version

**Why batch feedback matters:** If you send feedback in 45 separate emails over two weeks, that's 45 revision rounds — not 1. Batching your feedback into a single, organized list per round keeps the project on schedule and on budget.

### What Counts as a Revision

✓ Color adjustments
✓ Font changes
✓ Layout tweaks  
✓ Swapping images or content in the same structure
✓ Small copy edits (a few sentences, not a rewrite)

### What Does NOT Count as a Revision (This is New Scope)

✗ New pages or sections not in the original scope
✗ New features or functionality
✗ Complete redesign of an approved direction
✗ Rewriting content from scratch
✗ Designing new assets (logos, icons, illustrations) not in the original scope
✗ "Actually, can we go in a completely different direction?"

**New scope = Change Order = New pricing.** This isn't about saying no. It's about making sure we're both clear on what the original agreement covers.

## What Happens After Round [2]

If you need more changes after the included [2] revision rounds:

### Option A: Additional Revision Round

**[$X per round]** — You send one more batch of feedback, we make those changes.

### Option B: Hourly Rate

**[$X/hour]** — We'll track time on additional changes and bill accordingly.

### Option C: Monthly Retainer

**[$X/month]** — If your project needs ongoing iteration, a retainer gives you priority access and a set number of revision hours per month.

We'll agree on the approach before any additional work begins.

## The Bottom Line

This policy isn't here to be difficult. It's here because:

1. **Clear boundaries = better work.** When we both know what's in scope, we both make better decisions.
2. **Your budget is real.** Unlimited revisions would mean unlimited bills. We don't want that for you.
3. **Projects need to ship.** Iteration is valuable. Infinite iteration is procrastination.
